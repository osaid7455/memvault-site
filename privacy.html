MemVault — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2, 2026

TL;DR: MemVault collects nothing. Every memory you save is stored locally in your own browser. Nothing is ever transmitted to us — we don't have servers to send it to.

What data MemVault handles

MemVault lets you save snippets of text ("memories") — content you select on web pages or type yourself — so you can insert them into AI chat services (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini). This text is stored using Chrome's storage.local API on your device only.

What we collect

  • No personal information
  • No browsing history
  • No analytics or telemetry
  • No cookies
  • No data is transmitted off your device by MemVault

Why MemVault requests its permissions

  • storage — to save your memories locally in your browser.
  • contextMenus — to offer "Save to MemVault memory" when you right-click selected text.
  • Access to chatgpt.com, claude.ai, gemini.google.com — to show the memory panel and insert your chosen text into the chat box on those sites. MemVault does not read your conversations.

Data sharing

We do not share, sell, or transfer any user data — we never receive it in the first place. Your memories exist only on your device, and you can delete them at any time from the extension popup or by removing the extension.

Exports

The Export feature saves your memories as a JSON file to your own computer. What you do with that file is entirely under your control.

Changes

If this policy ever changes (for example, if an optional sync feature is added), the change will be described here with a new effective date before it takes effect.
